What jobs that require typing skills?

2 minute read

 

Typing skills is advantage, as technology becomes more commonplace in the workforce, therefore most jobs require at least the most basic typing skills. In fact, many employers are requiring candidates to be able to type at least a minimum of 35 wpm just for the sake of productivity.

Typing skills

Data Entry (Excel, or spreadsheet)

Data entry is simply taking data provided by a company and entering it into some sort of system or spreadsheet. This could be entering payroll items into a system for employers, catalog items into a system for retailers, and compiling lists of customer interactions for businesses, just to name a few.

Employers that hire people for data-entry positions usually look for applicants who can type between 60 and 80wpm. They want people who can enter data quickly so that they can input more information in a shorter amount of time. Along with speed, you’ll need to be accurate as you enter information into the computer system.

Word Processors (Word, PDF, document)

The position of word processors or typists requires the typist to accurately type 55 to 90 words per minute. This is an excellent opportunity for people who can type fast. The job usually requires an individual to convert hand written documents into digital copies by typing them manually on the computer. Documents can range anything from reports, meeting minutes or correspondence material.

Transcription (Subtitle, create script from recording)

Transcription is another type of work from home job that is interchangeably used with data entry and typing jobs. At the most basic level transcription is simply typing audio versions of documents. This could be lectures, workshops, conference calls, podcasts, and more.

Transcriptionists are required to have fast typing speed and excellent listening skills. The job demands the individual to write simultaneously with audio recordings while maintaining maximum accuracy.

Translation (From foreign language to native language)

Translators are required to have an in depth knowledge about the English language and the language they will be translating from.

Journalism/General Writing

Fast typing and reading skills benefit journalists, writers, authors and reporters significantly. It helps the individuals create reports and articles quickly and efficiently. Given that the field of journalism and general writing depend a lot on meeting short deadlines, fast typing skills can enable them to meet these deadlines without pulling too much effort.

Software/Web/Application Developers

Web languages, software coders or web developers are required to have fast typing skills with considerable accuracy. Fast typing skills enables the developer to create codes quickly and efficiently.

Legal Transcriptionists

Unlike general transcriptionists, legal transcriptionists work exclusively on legal documents. They are responsible for creating documents required by paralegals, attorneys and anyone else in the legal department.

Court Reporter

A court reporter needs to catch every word that is said during a court proceeding. This means that he or she needs to be able to type incredibly fast, around 225wpm. To achieve this, court reporters use special typing machines called stenotype machines. The keys on these machines are laid out differently than they are on a QWERTY keyboard, which is the standard computer keyboard layout. Court reporters go through special training to learn how to achieve such fast typing speeds.

Finally, typing skills is advantage

Even if work in a profession that doesn’t require you to have a minimum typing speed, it can be to your advantage to be able to type quickly. Having typing skills can help you accomplish your work more effectively when you do need to type. Having a fast typing speed can also make you more marketable in the job field.
